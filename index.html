Panels pressed from the cardboard your jobsite throws out.

MetaComb turns reclaimed corrugated cardboard into a patented honeycomb composite core — lighter than incumbent board materials, engineered in the USA for decorative and structural use.

Impressions Product overview How it's made

MetaComb™ material is a sustainable construction material offering a combination of function, pleasing aesthetics and relative low cost. MetaComb™ technology and product mitigates adverse environmental impacts by off-setting resource-demanding traditional building products and systems made out of wood and other non-renewable sources.

Environmental benefits from repurposing an environmentally troublesome waste material into a value-added, sustainable building material and product are great. The manufacturing process used in the production of MetaComb™ material is low-cost, straight forward and easily-scalable.

MetaComb™ products can be easily engineered and integrated into an existing material and/or building systems. Also, the material handling does not require any additional training or skills then working with plywood, or other composites. Moreover, there are a wide range of complimentary materials and/or systems available now, and projected in the future, that can be integrated into our product.

How it's made

MetaComb's process was born from watching corrugated cardboard pile up at construction sites — a patented way to turn it into a structural material instead of landfill waste.

Request the spec sheet
01
Source. Reclaimed corrugated cardboard is collected instead of routed to landfill or a conventional recycling stream.
02
Form. The cardboard is shaped into a honeycomb core using MetaComb's patented manufacturing process.
03
Laminate. The core is bonded to facing skins — resin sheets for decorative panels, structural facings for CSIP.
04
Finish. Panels are trimmed to size and finished for interior decorative use or structural assembly.

Questions

What architects, builders, and fabricators usually ask us first.

What's the difference between the two product lines? +

Decorative Resin Panels are lightweight and translucent, built for interior finish work. CSIP is a rigid, load-bearing version of the same honeycomb core, built for wall partitions and modular assemblies.

Is CSIP rated for load-bearing walls? +

CSIP is engineered for load-bearing and sound-insulated wall assemblies. Bring your structural engineer in early — final ratings depend on core thickness, facing material, and assembly design.

Where is MetaComb material made? +

MetaComb is developed and produced in the United States, out of Boston's Seaport District.

Is the manufacturing process patented? +

Yes. MetaComb holds a utility patent covering its manufacturing process, with additional patents in progress.

Can I get a sample before ordering? +

Yes — MetaComb currently takes small-batch orders and can provide samples of both product lines. Reach out to the team to arrange one.
