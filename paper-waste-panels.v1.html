03 / How it's made

Four steps, one fibre, no new trees

The process stays close to how paper itself is made — we just stop before it becomes a sheet you'd write on, and press it into something you can build with.

Read the full spec sheet
01
Source. Offcuts and misprints are collected directly from partner paper mills before they reach a landfill or incinerator.
02
Pulp. Fibre is re-pulped with water only — no bleach, no virgin pulp blended in to "improve" the look.
03
Press. A starch binder is added and the pulp is heat-pressed into the pattern die: Ridge, Fold, or Grain.
04
Cure. Panels air-cure for 72 hours, are trimmed to size, and are ready to ship flat.
04 / Questions

Before you specify it

The things architects and contractors actually ask us, in the order they usually ask them.

Panels are rated for interior partition and surface use. They are not a load-bearing structural material — treat them the way you'd treat a rigid MDF or fibreboard panel.

It tolerates humidity fine. Standing water or direct exposure will swell the fibre over time, so we don't recommend it for wet-room floors or exterior use without a sealant.

Standard woodworking tools — track saw, router, CNC. It fixes with construction adhesive, concealed clips, or screws with a countersunk cap depending on the pattern.

Because tone comes from the pulp itself rather than a surface coating, scratches and edge-wear show the same color underneath instead of a different substrate.

Stocked tones ship in 5–7 business days. Custom sizing or a tone outside the standard 15 adds roughly 3 weeks for a pulp run.
