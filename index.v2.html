03 / How it's made

From jobsite cardboard to a load-bearing core

MetaComb's process was born from watching corrugated cardboard pile up at construction sites — and building a patented way to turn it into a structural material instead of landfill waste.

Request the spec sheet
01
Source. Reclaimed corrugated cardboard is collected instead of routed to landfill or a conventional recycling stream.
02
Form. The cardboard is shaped into a honeycomb core using MetaComb's patented manufacturing process.
03
Laminate. The core is bonded to facing skins — resin sheets for decorative panels, structural facings for CSIP.
04
Finish. Panels are trimmed to size and finished for either interior decorative use or structural assembly.
04 / Questions

Before you specify it

What architects, builders, and fabricators usually ask us first.

Decorative Resin Panels are lightweight and translucent, built for interior finish work. CSIP is a rigid, load-bearing version of the same honeycomb core, built for wall partitions and modular assemblies.

CSIP is engineered for load-bearing and sound-insulated wall assemblies. Bring your structural engineer in early — final ratings depend on core thickness, facing material, and your assembly design.
